Welcome to Naawi-Oodena

Welcome to Naawi-Oodena, we are a community based in treaty 1, Winnipeg, Manitoba. Naawi-oodena meaning “centre of the heart and community” in Anishinaabe, is our 160-acre diverse community! With usis led by Treaty One Nation and Canada Lands Company, Our area has done what no others have, it is the biggest indigenous economic zone in all of Canada! Our vibrant community integrates residential, commercial, cultural, and recreational spaces, all rooted in Indigenous design principles and sustainability, although again, our community is vibrant with many varying cultural linings that are also included throughout our community!

Residential Living

This is our residential Living sector of our website! Naawi-Oodena offers a range of housing to accommodate our citizens diverse needs:

Low-Density Housing

We include affordable and inclusive low-density housing; from luscious large lake houses that include up to 3 stories, walk out decks, pools, and even patios, to our smaller more affordable but yet still single-family townhouses, including lots of space and intricate kitchen designs for families looking for spacious living quarters.

Medium-Density Housing

If you are still interested in living here but you may not have the financial capability to support this type of housing, we still also have a medium density housing all over our community. From high rise apartments that may be small but offer amazing views, to our more spacious stacked townhouses that include balconies with spectacular internal support.

Commercial spaces

When talking about Naawi-Oodena's commercial spaces, we are talking about the heart of our community; and we have 3 catagories:

Economic Opportunities

Naawi-Oodena has developed atleast1.2 million square feet of commercial space so far, including the adoption of economic growth and job creation. with this thousands of people have found employment in our community, maybe you want to be next!??

Integrated design

Our commercial areas are seamlessly integrated with residential zones, promoting walkability, eco friendly cycling and community interaction. The design emphasizes Indigenous architectural elements and includes cool artwork from our indigenous designing team.

Business Diversity

From large retail shops and restaurants to office spaces and community services, the mixed-use areas are designed to encapture a variety of businesses, supporting a vibrant local economy for all to use.
