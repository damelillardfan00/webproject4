Community Spaces

Green and Open Spaces

The development includes amazing luscious parks, vibrant plantacious community gardens, and our amazing high-tech asphalt walking trails, encouraging outdoor activities, dog walking and community gatherings.

Community Centers

Facilities are planned for events from all cultures, workshops, and meetings, serving as local hubs for social interaction and cultural exchange/celebration.

Public Art and Culture

Public spaces will feature Indigenous art installations and cultural markers, celebrating the rich/courageous heritage of the Treaty One Nations.

Sports & Recreation

Recreational Facilities

Naawi-Oodena hosts sports complexes, fitness centers, and recreational areas to promote active lifestyles among residents. We also include high-tech basketball parks with auto rebound machines!

Inclusive Programs

Programs are designed to be inclusive, applying to all age groups and abilities, fostering community engagement through sports and recreation such as basketball, soccer, volleyball, hockey, lacrosse, baseball, football, gymnastics, and squash.

Sustainable Design

Facilities incorporate sustainable materials and energy-efficient systems like solar panels, aligning with our community’s environmental goals.

Cultural campus & Education

Learning Opportunities

Throughout our community, we have included various schooling opportunities, whether that's in-school activities or outdoor tutoring, such as Kumon. We’ve dedicated plenty of space for school campuses that all will enjoy, including sports fields and fun gathering areas like picnic benches.

Honoring History

To give background on all cultures, we have also incorporated a historic museum to educate future generations about the past and celebrate our diverse roots.

Expression and Growth

Our cultural campuses provide space for all individuals—especially youth—to learn about today’s world and past events, while freely expressing themselves in a safe and inclusive environment.

Mixed-Use Village

Integrated Living

The Mixed-Use Village combines residential, commercial, and cultural spaces, creating a dynamic environment where people can live, work, and socialize. As said before.

Design Excellence

Emphasizing Indigenous design principles, the village features pedestrian-friendly streets, communal spaces, and sustainable architecture.

Community Engagement

This village model fosters deep community connections through shared spaces, local events, and inclusive development that reflects the values and identity of the people who live here.
